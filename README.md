# THE WRATH OF MORRISWORM 404 OG VERSION
**by Timothy Mills, Thomas Carlsen, and Vincent Gaglione**

This is the version of the game that we demo'd for our FINAL presentation project in our "Into to Game Development course... It was a lot of fun to make! though it is insanely unpolished, as we made the entire thing in a week. my goal is to make it more fun and enjoyable
during the summer of 2026. There are many things I wanted to add to it that we didn't have the time to implement, such as new enemies, I wanted to finish the ost, etc etc...

Presenting this game was a blast full of laughs because we were the only group that had an opening cutscene, which was an interesting concept in hindsight come to think of it. During that presentation however, we noticed VARIOUS glitches, or parts where the game was a little too merciless without reward for that level of risk...

**there are a lot of things that I want to change in the remaster, that will come out at the end of the summer**
for more information, go to https://timcinematicuniverse.com/html/blog.html to check out my blog!




# THE DEVS OF MORRISWORM 404 OG VERSION

**for this project, we had to give our group a name... and we came up with "CHROMERUBBLE STUDIOS" ... pretty funny, right?**

**I, Tim, the one uploading this**, was in charge of sound-design as a whole, I wrote the characters / story, wrote the dialogue, story-boarded the entire intro on a whim / Wrote the soundtrack in FL Studio with whatever spare time I had, and aided in the initial programming. I remember being on a huge time crunch, we worked on this so last-second that I had other finals that needed my attention... meaning this project had a bit less of my attention, as you could tell by the *VERY JANK* intro cutscene. I think the audience bought that jankness as intentional though, so, I still laugh about it to this day.

**Tom** was majorly responsible for just about every visual art asset that you could see in this project. I was honestly pretty floored at how great of a job Tom did with what little of a portfolio he had. Dude is just a genuine talent, mostly draws as a hobby, but these assets are just totally genuinely industry grade level to me. We have a game document somewhere that he dominates in. A pleasure to work with, and I got in touch with him recently for the remaster.

**Vin** lead most of the programming. He was the one to provide a rough-draft of every script in this project, which was then over-seen by both Tom and I. Another really cool thing that Vin did was print us physical copies of the "final" demo game. He created custom-covers and inserted them into old wii game-cases, and the whole thing was just really, really friggin' sick. I will post them on twitter at some point near the remaster's release
